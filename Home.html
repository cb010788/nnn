Sea Turtles

Sea turtles are fascinating creatures that inhabit tropical and subtropical seas worldwide. Sea turtles have a distinctive physical appearance with their large, air-breathing bodies, and shells that consist of an upper part called a carapace and a lower section called a plastron. There are seven species of sea turtles, belonging to two families, Dermochelyidae and Cheloniidae.

Sri Lanka is home to five of the seven species of sea turtles found in the world, including the Green Turtle, Loggerhead Turtle, Olive Ridley Turtle, Hawksbill Turtle, and the Leatherback Turtle. Each year, sea turtles come ashore to nest on the beaches of Sri Lanka, with nesting sites located primarily along the southern and eastern coasts of the island.

Unfortunately, sea turtles in Sri Lanka face several threats, including habitat loss, poaching, and accidental entanglement in fishing gear. However, conservation efforts are in place to protect these species. The Sri Lanka Turtle Conservation Project, for example, works to protect sea turtle nests, educate local communities about sea turtle conservation, and monitor turtle populations.

Turtles Nesting

Sea turtles lay their eggs in a nest they dig in the sand with their rear flippers. The group of eggs is called a clutch. They usually lay 100-125 eggs per nest and will nest multiple times, about two weeks apart, over several months.

Raine Island is the planet's largest remaining nesting site for endangered green turtles. As part of our world-leading conservation program on the island, the Raine Island Recovery Project has doubled the available nesting area for green turtles, enabling more females to nest successfully.

Sri Lanka is an important nesting site for sea turtles. The nesting behavior of each species differs slightly, with some species nesting at night, while others nest during the day. Green turtles nest all year round in Sri Lanka, with the highest abundance reported in Kosgoda and Rekawana. Turtle hatching season in Sri Lanka is known to be from November–May. Dikwella beach

Dikwella beach Sri Lanka or better know as turtle beach Sri Lanka, where sea turtle watching is popular, is among the top 10 Eco-friendly destinations named by most local and foreign travellers. Dikwella beach is not only one of the best places to spot sea turtles only in Sri Lanka but also in the world. In Dikwella you can observe the ancient ritual of turtles coming ashore and laying eggs. Dikwella beach is visited by five different species of sea turtles, which lay their eggs in the sand every night throughout the year.

Hikkaduwa beach

Hikkaduwa Sri Lanka is a popular turtle nesting destination on the west coast of Sri Lanka. Depending on the season, Hikkaduwa can be considered as the best place in the Sri Lanka to catch a glimpse of these great turtles or maybe have a swim with them. The waters around the Hikkaduwa are resided by many green turtles and loggerhead turtles. So, swimming with sea turtles is a very popular activity among travellers in Hikkaduwa. Watching sea turtles in their natural habitat is an incredible experience.

Mirissa beach

The beautiful beach town of Mirissa is one of the best places to see turtles in Sri Lanka.With a scenic coastline, plenty of great viewpoints and crystal clear water, it’s the perfect place to search for these elusive creatures. When it comes to swimming with turtles in Sri Lanka, one of the top locations is the famous Coconut Hill, which is located at the very far end of Mirissa Beach. We can spot them by swimming under the comming up waves. It’s an incredibly peaceful area to swim with turtles beacause of the pretty calm ocean.
