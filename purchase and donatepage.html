For more than 150 million years, oceans have been home to turtles around the world. Today, they are threatened by various factors and the rapidly changing marine environment. Pollution, plastic waste, ghost nets and oil spills are among the many factors that threaten the sea turtle's survival. As the Department of Coastal Conservation and Coastal Resource Management, we are here to help protect endangered turtles by conserving turtles and breeding them in turtle hatcheries. So, your donation helps us deliver on our Purpose, Turtles for Tomorrow. Our activities include advocacy, campaigns, education, research and our efforts to support rescue and rehabilitation.

About us

  • Our main responsibility is to monitor and try to conserve the endangered turtles. We are using advanced remote technologies such as satallite waves, radio waves to detect them.

    Therefore, Therefore, by learning more about sea turtles through this website, we hope to strengthen the world fund to protect the global turtle population.

Our Goals

  • As the Department of Coast Conservation and Coastal Resource Management, protecting sea turtles that come to our beaches is one of our main priorities.

    Treating injured turtles and breeding them to maintain the turtle population are also some of our goals.
