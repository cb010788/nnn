Turtle Hatcheries

Turtle hatcheries are not sanctuaries, zoos, rescue or rehabilitation centres. They are used only when natural nesting conditions are threatened, such as by flooding, erosion, predation or heavy poaching. So, these well-managed tourism contributes to the conservation of sea turtles. Currently, six out of the seven species of which are classified as vulnerable or endangered. However, for sea turtle conservation programmes to be successful, strict guidelines must be followed. There must be no interference in the nesting process, and numbers of tourists and/or volunteers must be tightly controlled to avoid distressing the turtles.

Kosgoda Turtle Hatechery

Kosgoda is the one of the very few hatcheries where all the five species in the country make their nests. They are the Olive Ridleys, Leatherbacks, Green Turtles, Loggerheads and Hawksbills.

Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery is one of 18 turtle hatcheries along the southern coast of Sri Lanka. The turtle hatchery is located in the south-western coastal village of Kosgoda, and it was established in 1981. It is also very popular, as all five species of turtles that visit Sri Lanka nest in Kosgoda.

Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery functions mainly as a rescue center and makes huge efforts to increase the rate of hatching and survival of baby turtles in their very initial stages. However, the hatchery also works on sick or injured adults, treating them and releasing them back into the ocean. They also run volunteer programs and awareness programs for local and international visitors.
