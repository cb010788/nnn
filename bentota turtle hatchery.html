Turtle Hatcheries

Turtle hatcheries are not sanctuaries, zoos, rescue or rehabilitation centres. They are used only when natural nesting conditions are threatened, such as by flooding, erosion, predation or heavy poaching. So, these well-managed tourism contributes to the conservation of sea turtles. Currently, six out of the seven species of which are classified as vulnerable or endangered. However, for sea turtle conservation programmes to be successful, strict guidelines must be followed. There must be no interference in the nesting process, and numbers of tourists and/or volunteers must be tightly controlled to avoid distressing the turtles.

Bentota Turtle Hatechery

Bentota Turtle Hatchery was started with a view to protect the sea turtles from extinction. Earlier, people, usually fishermen, used to collect the turtle’s eggs and sell it to whoever would pay the higher price. So, with the commencement of the turtle hatchery, these eggs were fed and cared for until they were 2 to 4 years old. We can find different species of sea turtles, such as Green Turtle, Leatherback and Olive Ridley in Turtle Hatcheries.

The Bentota Turtle Hatchery is a popular attraction in Bentota for all travelers. The pristine golden shores that draw you to our island also provide an enticing nesting ground for several endangered turtle species, from the Olive Ridley to the Leatherback turtle. The project aims at conserving and protecting the eggs from predators and other dangers. By visiting the Bentota turtle hatchery, you can learn more about these endangered, graceful creatures of the sea.

You can see turtle eggs which are safely buried in the sand in Bentota turtle hatchery. Turtles are held really gently with great attention to their proper development so that they can later be safely released into the sea where they can live in their natural habitat.

About us

Our Goals

  • As the Department of Coast Conservation and Coastal Resource Management, protecting sea turtles that come to our beaches is one of our main priorities.

    Treating injured turtles and breeding them to maintain the turtle population are also some of our goals.
