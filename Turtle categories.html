Turtle
Categories

Sea turtles are reptiles remarkably suited to life in the sea. Their hydrodynamic shape, large size, and powerful front flippers allow them to dive to great depths and swim long distances. There are seven species of sea turtle: the green sea turtle, Hawksbill sea turtle, loggerhead sea turtle, olive ridley sea turtle, Kemp’s ridley sea turtle, leatherback sea turtle, and flatback sea turtle. Sea turtles are among the oldest creatures on Earth and have remained essentially unchanged for 110 million years.

Green Sea Turtles


The Green sea Turtles are the most common turtle type in Sri Lankan waters. They are growing to a maximum length of 1m and weighing about 250kg. An adult female can lay between 40 to 120 eggs at once.

Hawksbill Sea Turtles


Hawksbill Sea Turtle which is a bit rarer than the Green Turtle. Comparatively, it is much smaller, reaching a maximum length of 90cm and weighing about 50 to 70kg. This one is renowned for its beautiful shell, which is made up of 13 symmetrical colorful pieces.

Loggerhead Sea Turtles


Loggerhead Sea Turtles are usually red and brown in colour and as their name suggests, is easily identifiable because of their large head. They are growing to a maximum size of 1m and weighing about 170 to 200kg.

Leatherback Sea Turtles


Leatherback Turtle, is the largest of the 5 species in Sri Lanka and sadly, is on the brink of extinction. This one is easily identifiable with its long front flippers and unique black and white stripy shell – its carapace or shell is in fact a layer of thin, tough, rubbery skin peppered with thousands of bone plates giving it a leathery appearance. It is in fact the only sea turtle that lacks a hard shell.

Olive ridley sea turtle


The olive ridley sea turtle, also known commonly as the Pacific ridley sea turtle, is a species of turtle in the family Cheloniidae. The species is the second-smallest and most abundant of all sea turtles found in the world.

Flatback Sea Turtles


The Australian flatback sea turtle is a species of sea turtle in the family Cheloniidae. The species is endemic to the sandy beaches and shallow coastal waters of the Australian continental shelf.

Kemp's ridley sea turtle


Kemp's ridley sea turtle, also called the Atlantic ridley sea turtle, is the rarest species of sea turtle and is the world's most endangered species of sea turtle. It is one of two living species in the genus Lepidochelys

About us

  • Our main responsibility is to monitor and try to conserve the endangered turtles. We are using advanced remote technologies such as satallite waves, radio waves to detect them.

    Therefore, Therefore, by learning more about sea turtles through this website, we hope to strengthen the world fund to protect the global turtle population.

Our Goals

  • As the Department of Coast Conservation and Coastal Resource Management, protecting sea turtles that come to our beaches is one of our main priorities.

    Treating injured turtles and breeding them to maintain the turtle population are also some of our goals.
